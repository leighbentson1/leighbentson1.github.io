Basic Lifts for Strength

These are lifts to focus on first. Think of them as your bread and butter...they'll be your foundation for WODs, strength, and gymnastics.

Bench Press

    Keep a tight grip on the bar
    Keep your chest up through the movement (thoracis extension)
    Elbows should be tucked

Strict Press

    Keep your elbows in tight - keep grip narrow
    Engage your lats throughout the movement
    Brace your core
    Keep ribs down

Back Squat

    Pull bar into back by activating lats
    Set hips back without bending over
    Focus on spreading knees apart, do not let them cave in
    Fill belly up with air, breathe out to expand stomach

Front Squat

    Create a "shelf" for the bar to sit on - do not rack too high
    Straight or slightly upward gaze - do not look down
    Stand straight up from bottom position to avoid getting "Stuck"

Deadlift

    Beginners should aim to lift their BW, intermediate 1.5x BW, advanced 2x BW
    Do not let back curl up
    Start with glutes low to the floor and stand straight up
    Save inverse grip for your heaviest lifts to build grip strength

Bent Over Row

    Keep the knees bent slightly
    Bring torso over the bar
    Keep hips high
    Pull bar toward your lower chest

Lunges

    Start with low weight to avoid injury (and sore glutes!)
    Keep chest up
    Place bar on your muscles, not spine
    Walk on rails for each foot, do not attempt to keep both feet in one straight line
